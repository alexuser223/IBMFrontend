Twilight Zone

The twilight zone can be found 200 to 1,000 meters below the ocean surface, at the point where the sun's rays can no longer reach. But , it's full of life including fish, crustaceans, jellyfish, squids and worms. Occasionally, there are bursts of bioluminescence , when living creatures give off their natural glows.

Midnight Zone

The midnight zone stretches from 1,000 meters to 4,000 meters below the ocean's surface. This region is shrouded in perpetual darkness, as sunlight cannot penetrate these depths. From bioluminescent creatures that light up the darkness to giant squids that roam the deep, the midnight zone is a world of wonders waiting to be discovered.

This zone if full of microbes which play an extensive role in the carbon cycle and eventual deep-sea carbon storage, thus understanding their activity is an essential step toward addressing climate change.

Abyss Zone

The abyssal zone, extending from about 3,000 to 6,000 meters below sea level, is one of the most remote and least explored regions of the ocean.

Challenges & Threats

Despite its remoteness, this zone faces significant environmental challenges and threats, primarily due to human activities. Two of the most pressing issues are deep-sea mining and pollution.
